we believe that every women has the right to a high-quality education.

Recent studies indicate that 2/3 of students who have student debt are women. In other words, women hold $890 billion of the country $1.4 trillion debt, while men only hold $490 billion. In a study conducted by Student Loan Hero, their findings that on average more women (28%) report feeling that their student debt in “unmanageable”, while only 13% of males feel the same way about their debt. Moreover, a shocking 48% of women report regret taking the loan out in the first place.

what are the consequences of student debt?

With the cost of tuition on the rise, and the number of students taking out loans also increasing, the student debt crisis has caught the nation’s attention. However, what many people don’t realize is that this issue is also a big gender-inequality issue.

Student debt carries many consequences. Not only does it place a massive unseen burden on the sholders of many young women in the United States, it also causes a great emotional toll on these very ambitious women, deterring them from pursuing their dreams and hindering their success.

our overarching goal is to encourage women to pursue higher education and unlock their full potential.

FEMPOW was started by a group of women who realized that this issue needs to be addressed and brought forth into the light. Our team wants to empower young women alike, and offer them a space in which they can get advice and guidance from women who have been in their shoes and experienced the same concerns as them. We offer a platform in which women can communicate with other women and voice their questions and concerns and get guidance and support from people who actually understand their pain, all while being in a safe space. We also, offer a service which generates a recommendation as to which company would be best to get a student loan from given the user’s situation.
